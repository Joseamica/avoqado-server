## Design Context

### Users

Avoqado serves two connected audiences across its frontends: venue owners, managers, and staff working in the Dashboard and TPV during time-sensitive business operations; and customers using QR, booking, checkout, and other public flows. Loading feedback must therefore feel fast and trustworthy in operational contexts while remaining friendly and recognizable in customer-facing experiences.

### Brand Personality

Original, organic, and energetic, while still dependable enough for payments and daily business operations. The logo suggests a seed becoming something alive: growth is the central emotional metaphor. The experience should feel contemporary and warm, never corporate-generic, childish, or ornamental.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use the Avoqado mark as a living system rather than as a static badge. The signature loading motion begins at the rounded tip of the green tail, travels upward, and draws the brand-green (`#7ADD2C`) body around the terracotta seed until it completes the recognizable silhouette. Favor transparent, self-contained assets that work on both light and dark surfaces. Avoid generic rotating spinners, continuous logo rotation, elastic/bouncy motion, visual noise, gradients, and effects that obscure the mark.

### Design Principles

1. Tell the tail-to-growth story in one clear signature motion, closing the mark around its seed.
2. Make feedback feel immediate: visible response within 100 ms and a calm loop around 1.4–1.8 seconds.
3. Preserve the logo silhouette and official brand colors across every product surface.
4. Keep the core loader dependency-free, scalable, and portable across web frameworks and native wrappers.
5. Animate only transforms, opacity, and SVG stroke properties; provide a meaningful `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback.
